The project is an integrated Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform designed to streamline and manage internal company resources. It includes features such as human resources management, attendance management, payroll management, leave systems, and audit systems, aiming to enhance operational efficiency and management effectiveness.

Challenges ⤵

What it takes to bring a new product from 0 to 1？

As the company continues to grow, we need to rapidly develop a brand-new integrated enterprise operation management platform. With a tight timeline and no support from a System Analyst (SA), all initial planning is handled collaboratively by the design and PM teams. For me, having no prior experience designing ERP systems, this is undoubtedly a challenging task but also a valuable opportunity for growth and learning.

My Role ⤵

As a UI/UX Designer, I took the lead on this project, managing the entire process from initial exploration to final implementation. This included user research, visual design, and front-end development. I closely collaborated with front-end engineers and product managers to develop feasible interface designs and interaction logic, ensuring consistency in design elements. Through iterative testing, I successfully balanced the platform's functionality and aesthetics.

Design Goals ⤵

After identifying project improvement needs, the focus shifted to design analysis, defining the information architecture, functional workflows, and design standards. A prototype framework was developed and applied with visual design, followed by usability testing and adjustments to optimize the product for a better user experience.

OPTIMIZED INFORMATION PRESENTATION

Integrate features and pages with clear information hierarchy to improve readability and clarity.

A CONSISTENT VISUAL STYLE

Create a unified design system and component library to reduce development costs and enhance consistency and usability.

CLEAR ACTION GUIDANCE

Streamline workflows by minimizing page transitions and excessive clicks, improving efficiency and user experience.

Design System ⤵

Based on the company's brand identity, blue and yellow were chosen as the primary colors.
The ERP system focuses on internal management operations, with the design approach emphasizing functionality and ease of use. As a result, the design style is defined as efficient, minimalist, and cohesive.

Overview ⤵

Identify and organize page and contextual elements, consolidating them into a unified component library. Design efforts prioritize leveraging these existing components while aligning closely with the information architecture. This approach reduces user learning curves, eases the workload for designers and engineers, lowers development costs, and ensures more efficient system maintenance.

Streamlined Approvals with One-Click and Batch Processing.

Simplify approvals with one-click functionality, making the process fast and efficient. Complete all workflows with ease, reducing complexity and minimizing errors. Support actions like approve, reject, add countersignatures, or reassign tasks, ensuring a more flexible and efficient approval process. The system automatically categorizes pending items and organizes them by submission time, enabling quick access to key documents and significantly improving administrative efficiency.

Instant Voice Connection for Enhanced Collaboration.

Simply connect via a mobile device or computer to make voice calls, reducing communication costs while enabling real-time team interactions. Whether discussing critical details or seeking support from teammates, the system offers reminders and confirmation features to ensure an efficient and seamless communication process, making collaboration smoother than ever.
